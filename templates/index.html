PCOS PREDICTOR

PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) is a hormonal disorder that affects women of reproductive age. It is characterized by hormonal imbalances, multiple cysts on the ovaries, and disrupted menstrual cycles. PCOS can have a significant impact on a woman's health and well-being


Symptoms of PCOS


Irregular Menstrual Cycles: Women with PCOS may experience irregular periods, with cycles that are longer or shorter than usual, or even missed periods.

Excess Hair Growth: PCOS can cause unwanted hair growth on the face, chest, back, or other areas of the body, known as hirsutism.

Acne: Many women with PCOS experience persistent acne that may be difficult to treat.


Weight Gain: PCOS is often associated with weight gain, especially around the abdomen, and difficulty losing weight.

Skin Darkening: PCOS can lead to patches of darkened skin, particularly in areas such as the neck, groin, or underarms.

Fertility Issues: PCOS is a leading cause of infertility due to irregular ovulation or the absence of ovulation.


Ovarian Cysts: Women with PCOS may have enlarged ovaries with multiple small cysts, which can cause pelvic pain or discomfort.

Fatigue: Women with PCOS may feel tired or experience low energy levels.

Mood Changes: PCOS can contribute to mood swings, anxiety, and depression in some individuals.
